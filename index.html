Jessica Del Rio


Software Engineer | UCSC '18

Hello, Jessica here! :D

This is my portfolio (My Requiem) of my software engineering journey. It is a chronological, descending ordering timeline of what I have done over the years.


I began my software engineering journey during high school by participating in many non-profit coding programs: CodeNow, Girls Who Code, Chick Tech, and Mission Bit. These organizations taught me how to build real-world applicable software. This helped me tremendously when I transitioned to UCSC for a Computer Science program.

During my college experience, I learned Computer Science fundamentals of data structures, runtime efficiency (Big O) and algorithms in depth. This was crucial in my SWE internship at Visa, where I re-built an enterprise data analytics service with privacy-complying insights sourced from aggregated card transaction data for Visa's merchant clients.

In 2018, I graduated from UCSC with my bachelor's in Computer Science. During Summer 2019, I was an intern for Bleacher Report and I built a backend service that monitors the health of their services. After my internship, I transitioned into a full-time role at Bleacher Report as a Backend Engineer. Where I collaborated with other backend Engineers to create an Elixir backend service that keeps track of Users’ text Posts and allows Users to upload images and/or videos to their Posts. We utilized Cloudinary to save and moderate theses media attachments.

Moreover, in December 2021, I was promoted to a Senior Software Engineer role, where I lead Backend Engineer Retrospective meetings and constructed actions items based on feedback to improve future sprints. In addition, I co-developed a new Elixir backend service that stores and creates the Rooms feature in the B/R app where mobile users can chat in real time on a current live video and see their crowd reactions.

Handy House

For my Web Applications class, three classmates and I built an online organizer to keep track of a household’s chores in one place. We used Vue.js as the frontend framework and used Python to make it run on Web2py. Our goal was to help communication between housemates. A user can create a house and add or delete members from the house. Then, users can add chores to the chore chart by pressing on the plus sign. When they make a chore, they can also assign someone to it and put a due date. In addition, they can edit or the delete the chores they made. Personally, I worked on the Back-End by checking if the user was already in a house or they would have to make a new house. If they had a house, I would also have to get their house's list and info. Overall, it was a fun project to work on because we did a lot of pair programming and bonded by learning Web2py and Vue.js.

Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program, Facebook Headquarters, Mountain View, CA

In our graduation, we pitched our project to our families and friends. It was an exciting day for me and my team because even though we thought it could not be possible, we successfully created a device.
